The big idea

Keep the left side sorted

Insertion Sort is excellent on tiny or nearly sorted arrays because it stops shifting as soon as the key is in the right spot.

i.

Choose a key

The next unsorted value is removed conceptually from the list.

ii.

Shift larger values

Walk left through the sorted prefix and make space by moving larger values right.

iii.

Insert

Place the key in the open slot. The sorted prefix grows by one.

When to use Insertion Sort

Use Insertion Sort for tiny arrays or data that is already close to sorted.

  • Small subarrays inside hybrid sorts such as TimSort or introsort variants.
  • Online insertion, where values arrive one at a time.
  • Simple stable sorting when implementation clarity matters.
